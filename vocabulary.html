The Indo-European Family

English belongs to the Germanic branch of the Indo-European family. It is a linguistic "cousin" to German, Dutch, and Swedish.

Key Timeline:
  • 5000 B.C.: Proto-Indo-European language exists.
  • 5th-7th Century: Old English (Anglo-Saxon) develops from West German settlers.
  • 11th Century: Middle English emerges after the Norman Conquest.
