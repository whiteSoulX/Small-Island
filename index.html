The Evolution Timeline

5000 B.C. - The Roots

English begins in the Germanic Branch of the Indo-European family, making it a cousin to German and Dutch [cite: 161, 175-177].

450-1100 A.D. - Old English

The language of Anglo-Saxon settlers mixed with Old Norse (Viking) invaders. This gave us basic words like House and Woman[cite: 201, 211].

1100-1500 A.D. - Middle English

Following the Norman Conquest, French influenced the language heavily. Words like Chivalrous and Judge entered here[cite: 202, 223].

17th Century - Global Spread

Colonization spreads English to North America (17th C) and later Australia (18th C), creating "World Englishes"[cite: 236].
